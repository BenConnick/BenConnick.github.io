"The game industry doesn't need another shooter; it needs something to inspire them"

The Legend of the Sword and Fairy is a Taiwanese role playing game released in 1995. When Journey creative director Jenova Chen played it, at the age of fourteen, it was the first piece of media to move him to tears. He says, in a 2012 interview with Eurogamer, “I decided that I wanted to dedicate my life to helping others experience what I had just felt”. For many people, Journey has done just that.

Chen often references a letter he received from a 15 year old girl. In brief, the letter explains that Journey changed her life, because it was the last game that she had played with her father before he died. She says that the game brought her joy in a time of grief, and helped her to understand her father’s own journey.

Journey has a powerful, resonant theme. The ability of a player to apply the metaphor of the Journey to their real experiences makes it a game that surpasses all cookie-cutter shooters and similarly shallow experiences. But that is not Chen’s concern here.

What Chen is talking about here is the fact that, whatever purpose shooters serve, it has been well filled, and inspiring experiences like Journey are far too few.

Parkin, Simon. "Jenova Chen: Journeyman." Eurogamer.net. Dx.net, 2 Apr. 2012. Web. 13 Sept. 2014.
