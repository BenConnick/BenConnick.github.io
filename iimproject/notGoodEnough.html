"my biggest complaint for computer games so far is they are not good enough for adults. For adults to enjoy something, they need to have intellectual stimulation ... You also need depth. You have the adventure -- the thrill of the adventure -- but you want the goosebumps too"




The above quote comes from a 2012 interview with Journey's creative director, Jenova Chen. Journey is revered by many game enthusiasts because it provides something that is greatly lacking among other games. Here, Chen is explaining what that something is. The implication of this quote is that games aren't living up to their potential. Often, when describing the different kinds of experiences that games can provide, an analogy will be drawn between blockbuster movies watched for entertainment, and movies that are watched to provoke thought.

Chen argues that adults are interested in more than simple entertainment. I interpret his use of the word "enjoy" here to imply that the experience has an lasting impact. I think what is interesting about his assertion, is that while it would seem to preclude games like Candy Crush, and Call of Duty, the experience of those games goes beyond the game itself, because of the social interactions that those games enable. The depth of a game like Candy Crush comes, arguably, from the interactions of its players outside of the game itself.

In Journey, much of the depth arises from the way that conventional clutter was stripped away to make the game a story about one or two people. In addition, the atmosphere and soundtrack of Journey allow it to produce what Chen calls here the "goosebumps" of depth. The music of Austin Wintory, along with the stunning visuals, set each chapter's mood perfectly, connecting the player deeply into an immersive experience. Because of all of this, a player can become fully absorbed into their discovery, as they explore a world that is at once alien and immediate.

Smith, Ed. "Gamasutra - A Personal Journey: Jenova Chen's Goals for Games." Gamasutra - A Personal Journey: Jenova Chen's Goals for Games. UBM Tech, 18 May 2012. Web. 13 Sept. 2014.
